⚠ Storm alert for Southport

Severe weather is expected

Do these now

  • Charge phones and backup batteries
  • Fill water jugs — and the bathtub
  • Set out flashlights (not candles)
  • Make sure you have a week of medications
  • Secure or haul boats; double your lines
  • Bring in anything that can blow away
  • Have a generator? Check fuel and give it a test run

If the power goes out

  • Keep fridge and freezer doors closed
  • Never run a generator indoors or in a garage
  • Stay far away from downed lines — call 911
  • Check on your nearest neighbor

Wildfire Risk

The Maine Forest Service rates fire danger daily during fire season (late March–late November): Low, Moderate, High, Very High, or Extreme. At Very High or Extreme, burn permits are often suspended — check the map below before burning brush or lighting a campfire.

Ticks in Maine can be active on any day above about 40°F, and mosquitoes can bite on days rated Low. A low estimate is never a reason to skip precautions during a walk in tall grass or brush.

How these estimates work — and how well

Each meter combines today's hourly forecast (temperature, humidity, and wind) with the seasonal calendar for Midcoast Maine. Mosquitoes are scored on the dusk hours, when they bite most; ticks on the daytime hours. Temperature sets the ceiling, and dry air or wind pulls the estimate down. The seasonal calendar follows the deer tick life cycle — the April–July nymph peak (when most Lyme transmission happens) and the October–November adult season — and the June–August mosquito peak.

What we checked: ten seasonal scenarios (January cold snap, July heat wave, a breezy dry summer day, mild October afternoon, and so on) were run through the model and compared against standard vector-ecology guidance — all ten land in the expected range. An earlier version failed this test and was corrected before publishing. On the day this was built, a live comparison against AccuWeather's mosquito index for Boothbay Harbor came out one notch apart in the same direction (theirs High, ours Very High) — expect this meter to run slightly hot on humid summer days.

What this can't tell you: weather is only part of the story. These estimates know nothing about actual insect populations near your house — standing water, deer traffic, leaf litter, or how wet the past month was all matter and aren't in the model. For real surveillance, use the sources below.

Drought & your well

Most Southport homes are on private wells, and dry summers are becoming more common. This map shows current drought conditions in Maine — it updates every Thursday.

  • Maine Drought Task Force ↗

    State drought updates — and the place to report a dry well, which helps Maine track where help is needed.

  • Maine CDC: Private Well Water ↗

    Private wells aren't regulated — testing is up to you. Test for bacteria and nitrates yearly, and arsenic, radon, and uranium every 3–5 years.

  • UMaine Extension: Well Water ↗

    Plain-language guidance on testing, treating, and protecting your well — including what to do if it runs low.
